## 3. Advanced Research Skills

### 3.1 Paper Reading & Analysis

**Paper Reading Curriculum**:

**Phase 1: Breadth** (Months 1-3)
- 50+ papers across 5 areas
- Focus: Understanding landscape
- Method: Skim, categorize, summarize
- Goal: Build mental model

**Phase 2: Depth** (Months 4-6)
- 20+ papers, deep reading
- Focus: Mastering specific area
- Method: Critical analysis, notes
- Goal: Become expert

**Phase 3: Critique** (Months 7-9)
- 10+ papers, critical evaluation
- Focus: Finding gaps, opportunities
- Method: Detailed notes, questions
- Goal: Identify research directions

**Reading List** (Essential Papers):

**Type Systems**:
1. "Types and Programming Languages" (Pierce)
2. "Dependent Types in Practical Programming" (Xi, Pfenning)
3. "Refinement Types for Haskell" (Vazou et al.)

**Distributed Systems**:
1. "The Byzantine Generals Problem" (Lamport et al.)
2. "Practical Byzantine Fault Tolerance" (Castro, Liskov)
3. "HotStuff: BFT Consensus with Linearity" (Yin et al.)

**Formal Verification**:
1. "Software Foundations" (Pierce et al.)
2. "seL4: Formal Verification of an OS Kernel" (Klein et al.)
3. "Kani: Verification for Rust" (AWS)

### 3.2 Research Methodology

**Experimental Design**:
- **Hypothesis**: Clear, testable
- **Variables**: Independent, dependent, controlled
- **Methodology**: Reproducible
- **Statistics**: Proper analysis

**Benchmarking Best Practices**:
- **Baseline**: Industry standard
- **Workload**: Representative
- **Metrics**: Relevant, measurable
- **Reporting**: Complete, honest

**Implementation Research**:
- **Prototype**: Minimal viable
- **Evaluation**: Comprehensive
- **Artifacts**: Public, reproducible
- **Documentation**: Complete

### 3.3 Academic Writing

**Paper Structure**:
1. **Abstract** (200 words): Problem, approach, results
2. **Introduction** (2 pages): Motivation, contributions
3. **Related Work** (2 pages): Literature review
4. **Approach** (4 pages): Technical details
5. **Evaluation** (3 pages): Experiments, results
6. **Discussion** (1 page): Limitations, future work
7. **Conclusion** (0.5 pages): Summary

**Writing Process**:
- **Week 1-2**: Outline, structure
- **Week 3-6**: First draft
- **Week 7-8**: Revision, feedback
- **Week 9-10**: Final polish
- **Week 11-12**: Submit, iterate

**Writing Resources**:
- "The Craft of Research" (Booth et al.)
- "Writing for Computer Science" (Zobel)
- "They Say / I Say" (Graff, Birkenstein)
